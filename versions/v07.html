The Andes are not simply tall. They are diverse. From the equatorial cloud forests of Colombia — dripping with orchids, alive with hummingbirds — to the hyper-arid Atacama Desert in Chile, where it has not rained in recorded history. From the high-altitude wetlands of Bolivia to the glacial fjords of Patagonia.

The range runs through eleven degrees north and fifty-five degrees south of the equator. In that span, it contains virtually every climate zone on Earth. Nearly one in six of all plant species on the planet grows somewhere on the Andes.

This is not a place. It is a world.

The Andes
Made the Amazon.

Every drop of water in the Amazon River — the world's largest river by volume — began as snow or rain on the Andes. The mountains catch moisture from the Atlantic, lift it, freeze it, and release it slowly. Without the Andes, there is no Amazon.

Without the Amazon, 10% of all species on Earth have nowhere to live.
